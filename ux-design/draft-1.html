Contracts now need a new kind of review.

Who may use AI systems?
What data may be submitted into them?
Can confidential information be used with third-party AI tools?
Who owns or can rely on AI-assisted output?
What representations, controls, notices, or audit rights are needed?

Ordinary business contracts may now contain exposure that was not obvious when the form was drafted.

A services agreement may not address whether AI tools can be used to perform the work. A confidentiality clause may not clearly restrict AI-based processing. An IP provision may not account for AI-generated output, model training, residual knowledge, or third-party AI systems. A data protection addendum may not match how vendors are actually using AI in their operations.

For lawyers, the challenge is not simply using AI to review contracts. The challenge is helping clients understand whether their existing contracts and templates are ready for the business world that AI is creating.

What changes

The risk is not always where the word AI appears.

AI-related issues often surface through familiar provisions: confidentiality, data use, IP ownership, subcontracting, audit rights, indemnity, limitation of liability, security, and compliance.

01

AI regulatory exposure

AI-related obligations are developing quickly across jurisdictions, industries, and customer expectations. Accordli Analyze helps surface those questions in the context of the agreement in front of you.

02

Contract template drift

Many templates were written before AI became part of daily business operations. Others have been updated in fragments, with isolated AI language added without a full interaction review.

03

Client confidence

Clients want to know whether they can use AI, whether their vendors can use AI, whether their data is protected, and whether their contracts support internal AI policies.

Different by design

Not a blank chatbot. Not loose commentary.

Accordli Analyze is a structured contract review workflow designed around lawyer-curated AI contract analysis.

Many lawyers have tried using general-purpose AI tools for contract review. They can be useful, but they also create serious problems for professional legal work.

They may produce inconsistent answers. They may miss issues unless prompted correctly. They may overstate conclusions. They may invent authority or reasoning. They may require the user to know exactly what to ask. They may create confidentiality and data-handling concerns.

Accordli is designed for repeatable review, legal judgment, client communication, and the specific contracting challenges created by AI adoption.

Confidentiality

Privacy and confidentiality are designed in.

Contracts are sensitive. Client policies are sensitive. Contract analysis is sensitive. Accordli Analyze is being built for confidential legal work.

01

Private workspaces

Your contracts and review materials belong to your account. They are not mixed into a shared customer environment for model training or cross-customer analysis.

02

Encrypted and access-controlled

Documents and analysis data are protected through encryption and access controls. Access is authenticated and tied to the appropriate account and user permissions.

03

No training on your data

Your contract text, client policies, uploaded documents, and review content are not used to train foundation models.

04

Zero-retention model processing

Where external model providers are used, Accordli is designed to use zero-retention processing so contract inputs are processed for the review and not retained by the model provider for training or reuse.

05

Audit trail

Accordli is designed to support a full audit trail of review activity, including uploaded agreements, generated findings, memo outputs, and user actions.

06

Built for professional review

The system is designed around the reality that lawyers must protect confidential materials while still working efficiently with modern tools.
